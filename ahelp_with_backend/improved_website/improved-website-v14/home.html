About the Program

AHELP (Arkansas Healthy Employee Lifestyle Program) is a state-sponsored initiative designed to encourage healthy habits, reduce healthcare costs, and improve the overall well-being of Arkansas state employees. The program provides tools, resources, and incentives to support physical, mental, and emotional wellness.

Participants track activities such as physical fitness, hydration, nutrition, and more â€” earning points toward tangible incentives like paid time off. It is accessible to all state employees and encourages cross-agency engagement through community challenges and progress tracking.

Legislative Foundation

Act 724 of 2005: Originally authorized the Arkansas Healthy Employee Lifestyle Program under the Department of Health and Human Services. It allowed state agencies to promote healthy behavior and offered paid leave as an incentive.

Act 444 of 2025: Transitioned AHELP to the Department of Transformation and Shared Services (TSS), updating the framework to align with current technology, modern wellness strategies, and integration with state employee benefits systems.

The program now supports integrated fitness tracking, personalized health risk assessments, agency-level reporting, and state-wide performance metrics â€” all with a focus on improving employee morale and public service efficiency.

Administrator Tools

AHELP administrators can manage user engagement and agency performance using advanced tools and reporting dashboards:

  • View real-time analytics per agency or full program overview
  • Manage users, reset passwords, and assign permissions
  • Approve or deny points redemption requests
  • Identify suspicious activity or trends through alerting systems
  • Export agency-specific or state-wide reports
  • Sync data from users' fitness devices
